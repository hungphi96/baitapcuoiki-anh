History of Vietnamese Calligraphy

Calligraphy originally meant a method of writing letters accurately and beautifully, but over time, calligraphy has gone beyond its original meaning and has become the art of stylized writing, creating artistic images. express the deep meaning of the author. Calligraphy originally came from China and was introduced to Vietnam in the early years of the Han Dynasty. The first Vietnamese calligraphy work to be found is the inscription "Đại Tuỳ Cửu Chân quận Bảo An đạo tràng chi bi văn", emblazoned with the word "triện" on the forehead of the stele in Truong Xuan village (Thanh Hoa). This Calligraphy work is currently on display at the history museum.

The history of the formation of the art of Chinese calligraphy in Vietnam is similar to calligraphy in China, but the aesthetic expression has relatively different points such as: The pen is soft but not weak and deep. no sediment, launch without frenzy. To be able to write calligraphy in Chinese characters, the writer needs to be knowledgeable about the meanings of the characters, have a deep knowledge of the combination and distribution of shapes, and shape the letters through each line.

Modern Vietnamese calligraphy art

Along with time, Quoc Ngu script was born with Latin letters, many Vietnamese people wanted to write beautiful letters and Vietnamese Latin calligraphy also began to appear from there. The appearance of modern Vietnamese calligraphy is a creative breakthrough in national art, when it is both innovative with the use of Latin letters and boldly traditional with an ancient art style. . Unlike the art of Chinese calligraphy, Vietnamese calligraphy is not within the framework but is more liberal and disruptive. Expressiveness in Vietnamese calligraphy art is quite clear, the writer's aesthetic as well as the taste of the audience is often towards simplicity, harmony, simplicity, poetic and romantic quality. The content in Vietnamese calligraphy is usually folk songs, proverbs, the teachings of famous people, lyrical poems... Each Vietnamese calligraphy work depends on the feelings and inner thoughts of the writer. created when it is a painting with gentle and elegant nuances, but sometimes it is a smoky and smoky painting ... making the viewer of the painting seem to be lost in the world of quiet and quiet art.

Vietnamese calligraphy is shown on many materials such as paper, cloth, wood, stone, fruit, bamboo paintings, mosaics and even on ceramics. Vietnamese calligraphy is popularized, going in two directions: The first direction, comes from the nostalgia of a time when the Chinese and Nom characters reigned. Artisans have expressed the form of Vietnamese characters (with Latin letters) in circular or square blocks of Chinese characters or in a "reversed" script that is easily readable only from the back of the page. The second direction, expressing the form of Vietnamese characters in the form of a script originally from the West used for the Latin alphabet system. In this trend there is a breakthrough creativity when combining the spirit of the brush with the national language handwriting to create the modern Vietnamese calligraphy art that we see today. In recent years, "Vietnamese calligraphy" and "Quoc Ngu" calligraphy (Latin script) have begun to flourish and become a fairly strong development movement.

Ways of writing in Vietnamese calligraphy

Letter "chân phương"

This is the most common writing style in Vietnamese calligraphy. letter "chân phương" is very convenient to write, easy to read, with clarity, clarity, and flexibility between different bold and light strokes. Because it is very convenient to write and read, people often use letter "chân phương" a lot, this type of script is very popular and strongly developed.

Letter "cách điệu"

This type of lettering is used to make letters to represent different images. You can also freely create and transform the words into the images that the writer wants to convey and describe. The feature of letter "cách điệu" is that it can deceive the viewer's eyes, because this is a work that is heavier on the image than on the text. Although letter "cách điệu" are difficult to write and also difficult to read, when looking at the words, you can easily feel the author's mind expressed through hidden images in the work.

Letter "Thảo"

"Thảo" is a Vietnamese calligraphy style that expresses the soul of each word more than the meaning of the words. When writing "Thảo", the writer can be very arbitrary and liberal, always containing a lot of the writer's inner strength. However, because the font is a bit arbitrary, it is often difficult for readers to recognize the font. Therefore, when viewers look at calligraphy works written in the letter "Thảo" style, it often takes a lot of time to be able to read the author's handwriting. There is even a case where the viewer cannot read the author's handwriting anymore. However, Vietnamese calligraphy works in the letter "Thảo" style are often very artistic and aesthetic and attract the attention of many viewers.

1. Calligraphy in China

Calligraphy in China is very developed

2. Calligraphy in Japan

Calligraphy in Japan is a favorite art and aesthetic discipline of many Zen masters. Therefore, Zen masters are the people who write the most calligraphy, this discipline is also known as calligraphy and is always associated with the art and aesthetics of Zen Buddhism. Japanese calligraphy often emphasizes the meaning of the words rather than the images, the characters are usually very liberal, free, and bold in the colors of many Japanese Zen masters.

Calligraphy in the West has different characteristics compared to calligraphy in Asian countries.
