The ancestor of Vietnamese calligraphy art

In the 1930s, when Vietnamese people only knew how to write calligraphy in Chinese characters, intellectuals with a deep passion for Vietnamese silently studied how to write calligraphy in the national language script.

According to Assoc. Prof. Dr. Vo Van Nhon, former deputy dean of the Faculty of Literature and Languages, University of Social Sciences and Humanities (Vietnam National University, Ho Chi Minh City), many writers in the past looked at the poet Dong Ho (1906-1969). ) was the one who laid the first bricks to build calligraphy in Vietnamese, according to the Latin alphabet. Having spent many years researching poet Dong Ho, also the author of the book Dong Ho - Mong Tuyet (Publishing House of Writers' Association, 1992), Mr. Nhon said that his contemporaries often came to ask the poet to write letters. Poet Quach Tan (1910-1992) once "asked" for Dong Ho's words for the mural to hang in the house. Writer Le Van Sieu (1911-1995) also mentioned: "Every New Year, Dong Ho sends you a card with a poem to celebrate spring". All are in the national language, but written with brush and ink in the style of Chinese calligraphy before. At first, many people found it strange, but later they were quite excited when they received the word from Dong Ho. Through the remaining documents and autographs, adding that Dong Ho was a person who devoted his life to the development of his mother tongue... many people consider Dong Ho as the "ancestor" in the Vietnamese calligraphy world.

"Four Pillars of Calligraphy"

Those who are passionate about the art of calligraphy will probably memorize the four words "Hoa, Bach, Nguyen, Com". That is the name of four famous Vietnamese calligraphers known as the "four pillars" of Vietnamese calligraphy: "Thanh hoằng khê" Le XUan Hoa, "Lỗ công" Nguyen Van Bach, "Vĩnh nguyên" lai Cao Nguyen, "Nam ba cầm văn" Cung Khac Luoc.

1. "Thanh Hoằng Khuê" Le Xuan Hoa.

Calligrapher Thanh Hoang Khe Le Xuan Hoa was born in Phu Khe village, Hoang Khue district (Thanh Hoa). He was born in 1914, in a family with a Confucian tradition, and learned Confucian characters from a young age.
He passed away on January 3, 2008, at the age of 95.
Many calligraphy works of Le Xuan Hoa have been included in the collection by the Vietnam Revolution Museum. Those are the letters expressing Ly Thuong Kiet's poem Nam Quoc Son Ha; the poem thrift and integrity of President Ho Chi Minh; In addition, the museum also preserves all his calligraphy writing tools such as: ink sharpening, brush, pen holder, Chinese books printed in the early 19th century. .

Calligraphy Thanh Hoang Khe has all four forms, but his forte is probably drafts and drafts. Calligraphy is the art of using the beauty of writing to express the truth and goodness of thought and soul. Therefore, calligraphers always attach great importance to the height of their thoughts and feelings. It is possible to immediately take eight words: ”Thiết thạch can trường, tinh anh tuyết ngọc” (The spirit is as strong as stone, the soul is as pure and beautiful as the snow like jade) to summarize the spiritual appearance of the painter Le. Xuan Hoa. It is the poetic poem ”Tuyên ngôn độc lập” by Ly Thuong Kiet or the verse ”Cáo tật thị chúng” by "Mãn giác thiền sư", as well as the poem of Thuong Son ”Cử đầu hồng nhật cận, đối ngạn nhất chi mai”.... All of them exude that strong and beautiful quality. The pen strokes are solid, decisive, and the ink color is dense in the beginning words, which can be said to be very typical for Le Xuan Hoa's writing style. The interesting thing is that besides Thu, Thanh Hoang Khe Le Xuan Hoa also has a painting, mainly a sketch.

Besides the calligrapher Thanh Hoang Khe, there is also a teacher - an old teacher - Le Xuan Hoa is still dedicated to teaching and passing on the profession to a class of people who love calligraphy, including students of "cổ lai hi"
Calligraphy Thanh Hoang Khe Le Xuan Hoa is a milestone in the history of Vietnamese calligraphy, a remarkable achievement of Han Nom calligraphy, a small but unique part that contributes to the appearance of the traditional culture of the country.

2. "Lỗ Công" Nguyen Van Bach.

Nguyen Van Bach (1924 - 11/11/2021) with the name Lo Cong, was a physician, calligrapher, and translator of Chinese studies. He was born in Nghia Hung commune, Gia Loc district, Hung Yen province, in a family of Han grapes. He learned Chinese characters and learned medicine from his father, a famous Confucian and physician. Growing up, he followed his father's footsteps, becoming a physician to treat people's diseases. For a time, he and his family lived in Hai Phong until 1959, when he took the exam and was recruited along with 28 other physicians to become a lecturer in the first oriental medicine class of the Central Institute of Traditional Medicine (now called is the Central Hospital of Traditional Medicine).

The talented hands of that calligrapher are not God-given. His father, a vinedresser, a healer, worked hard for his son since childhood. He said: “My word is also temporary, not comparable to the word my great-grandfather. The old man's words are as beautiful as dragons and phoenixes dancing, famous near and far." When he was a boy, he learned to write by dipping a pen in water to write on a brick background, then when he was mature, he used ink and paper to write. Later, when he worked in Eastern medicine, he had more conditions to be exposed to Chinese characters. In his spare time, he practiced writing, sometimes he also dreamed of words at night. He gives the word who, always find out what that person matches, when it hangs, it makes sense.

To write a favorite word, it is not always possible to write. You have to think about that word a lot, wait for a cool day, relax your soul, burn incense, drink a glass of slightly ivory wine, then wear your pen as if you're dropping your soul in it. Think long, write very fast. Those words always have a god.
Writing is just a hobby. He explained: Writing is fast, but thinking takes longer. Must calculate the size of each pen stroke to balance the lines. Moreover, you must immerse yourself in the word, live in the word, and absorb the beauty of the word in both form and content. Each calligraphy must show the spirit of the writer, if not just a "drawing of words".

3. "Vĩnh Nguyên" Lai Cao Nguyen.

Scholar, calligrapher Vinh Nguyen - Lai Cao Nguyen was born in 1929 in Lam Khe hamlet, An Binh commune (now Dong Kinh commune) Dong Quan district (now Dong Hung district) Thai Binh province. Born into a family with a tradition of studiousness, he learned Confucianism at an early age. Being gifted, he easily absorbed the instruction of his father and uncle.
At the time when Chinese characters began to lose importance, the Confucian examinations were gradually replaced by French New School, Lai Cao Nguyen still spent a lot of time studying, researching and studying ancient Han books. Vietnamese poetic works written in Han Nom script. Because he thinks this is the "key" to deeply understand the quintessence of the country's literature of sages such as Ba Huyen Thanh Quan, Nguyen Du, Nguyen Trai,...
He was a literate Confucian, studied Chinese characters from the age of 6, joined the resistance war, and after 3 years of training in linguistics in China. In 1953, he was sent to study in Nanning, China, studied Chinese Pedagogy and then returned to work as a lecturer at History Department - Hanoi University. In 1957, he was a lecturer of Chinese at the Faculty of Foreign Languages, Hanoi National University of Education. Later, as Dean of the Chinese Department - University of Foreign Languages ​​(University of Foreign Languages), he participated in researching and compiling many books, textbooks, Vietnamese - Chinese dictionaries, "hán nôm".

Mr. Lai Cao Nguyen is considered a calligrapher with a contemplative style, erudite study, extensive knowledge of Han Nom script. He once shared: “The art of calligraphy written in Han Nom requires the writer to be very delicate, with a heart and a mind. There is a gentle handwriting like a gentle breeze, gentle but free from vulgarity. There is a strong, resilient feature like to suppress evil, immediately erase evil, evil has not arisen in the mind... Not only is it a common elegant pleasure, this art is also a place for people to enjoy. The calligrapher conveys Eastern philosophies, conveys his feelings, emotions, and profound outlook on life. For Mr. Nguyen, giving the first letter of the year is a long-standing traditional cultural beauty of the Vietnamese people. Each word is drawn on paper like "pearls" conveying the thoughts and feelings of the writer. As the editor and translator of many reference books such as: The Sino-Vietnamese dictionary, the Sino-Vietnamese handbook, The History of Primitive Culture...Teacher Lai Cao Nguyen is still diligently researching every day, for many years helping generations of students who love to learn about the art of writing Han Nom characters. He also collaborates with leading experts and staff from the Institute of Han Nom and Linguistics at universities and colleges across the country to advise and organize many seminars and exhibitions. , dialogue displays Han Nom characters, attracting many young people to visit. It can be said that Han Nom calligraphy is the predestined relationship that is the driving force for Vinh Nguyen - Lai Cao Nguyen to practice hard, reach the level of "fine calligraphy", constantly create and give life a beautiful painting. .

4. "Nam ba cầm văn" Cung Khac Luoc.

With Dr. Cung Khac Luoc, in addition to the traditional rules and regulations, he is also famous as a calligrapher in a disruptive style with a liberal layout, clearly showing the author's talent and romance.
From an early age, the boy Luoc was taught the Quoc Ngu script, the Nho script, the Nom script and the French language. When he grew up, he studied under Le Dai and Tu Long - the key figures of the Dong Kinh Nghia Thuc movement. After graduating from Hanoi National University of Education, he was assigned to the Viet Bac Autonomous Region to build the school.


Each calligrapher always has two sympathetic people, that is, the brain and intellect of the giver meets the heart and soul of the recipient. "Calligraphy" is "magic of words".
The art of calligraphy requires the writer to have both hands and fingers, and to understand the deep and profound meaning of the word, so that the spirit of the word and the personality of the pen holder can be expressed.
With Dr. Cung Khac Luoc, in addition to the traditional rules and regulations, he is also famous as a calligrapher in a disruptive style with a liberal layout, clearly showing the author's talent and romance. There were times when he was dressed in a red brocade robe, sitting solemnly like a monk in a commentary session, sometimes wearing an old dark robe, a brown beanie on his head, leather sandals on his feet, floating on each step. The page on the corner of the street seems to forget everything in the world...
In the air full of spring, the calligrapher Cung Khac Luoc is still busy launching pen on each page, bringing to life talented handwriting, containing thoughts, experiences and even wishes in a new spring. - Spring of human life, of faith and hope.
